Care conditions and soil

Apple trees thrive best in deep, nutrient-rich soils that are provided with humus.

Cold, wet locations lead to poor tree and fruit development and increased disease infestation. It is important to have a location that is not at risk of late frost, as the open apple-blossom can only tolerate minus one degree Celsius.

Fruit trees have to be cut regularly in order to develop new fruit trees and to be freed of old wood. If the apple tree is cut too late in the year, it may no longer sprout properly and bear significantly less fruit. The first cut is therefore made in the spring on a mild day without rain, as the branches are no longer too brittle and not many buds have sprouted yet. When pruning in spring, it is important to ensure that temperatures are above -5 ° C at night in order to promote wound healing.
